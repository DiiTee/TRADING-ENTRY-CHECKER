WHEN TO DISPLAY

Only calculate and display Fibonacci when ALL of the following are true:

• A clear directional trend exists.
• A completed swing high and swing low can be identified.
• The current structure is suitable for Fibonacci analysis.

Do NOT display Fibonacci during:

• Sideways markets
• Choppy/range-bound structure
• Immature price discovery
• Incomplete swings
• Low-confidence structure where Fibonacci would be unreliable

FIBONACCI CALCULATION (MARKET CAP)

When Fibonacci analysis is applicable, use Market Cap (MC) instead of token price.

1. Identify a valid completed bullish swing.

• Swing Low MC = Lowest Market Cap of the completed swing.
• Swing High MC = Highest Market Cap of the completed swing.

2. Calculate the swing range:

Range = Swing High MC − Swing Low MC

3. Calculate the Fibonacci retracement levels:

0.500 = Swing High MC − (Range × 0.500)

0.618 = Swing High MC − (Range × 0.618)

0.786 = Swing High MC − (Range × 0.786)

4. Define the Golden Entry Zone:

Upper Boundary = 0.500 level
Preferred Level = 0.618 level
Lower Boundary = 0.786 level

5. Compare the Current Market Cap (MC) against the Golden Entry Zone and determine whether it is:

• Above the Golden Zone
• Inside the Golden Zone
• Near the 0.618 retracement
• Below the Golden Zone

6. Compare the existing Safe Entry Range (MC) with the Golden Entry Zone and determine whether they overlap.

This calculation is for informational context only and must NOT modify the existing Entry Range, Safe Entry Range, Structure Score, Structure Grade, Confidence Tier, or Structure Verdict.
