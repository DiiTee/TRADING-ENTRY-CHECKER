==================================================
SECURITY BRIEFING REWORK
==================================================

CURRENT PROBLEM

The section currently outputs two separate risk systems:

1. Cabal Risk Badge:
- LOW RISK
- MEDIUM RISK
- HIGH RISK

2. Security Tier:
- Tier A
- Tier B
- Tier C
- Tier D

These can contradict each other.

Example:

SECURITY / CABAL BRIEFING [LOW RISK]

Security Tier: C
Score: 14/28
Verdict: HIGH RISK

This creates confusion because users see two different overall risk assessments.

--------------------------------------------------
REQUIRED CHANGE
--------------------------------------------------

Security Tier becomes the ONLY master security rating.

Remove the old headline risk badge:

[LOW RISK]
[MEDIUM RISK]
[HIGH RISK]

Replace with:

Security Tier: B
Score: 21/28 (75%)
Verdict: MODERATE RISK

The Security Tier and Verdict must be treated as the official overall security assessment.

IMPORTANT SEPARATION OF RESPONSIBILITIES
==================================================

Holder Density and Holder Efficiency measure:

- Adoption quality
- Ownership quality
- Distribution health
- Concentration risk

These metrics belong to Distribution Security.

Do NOT place them inside:

- Launch Coordination Risk
- Bundle Concentration Risk
- Wallet Cluster Confidence
- Self-Snipe Evidence

Those sections measure coordination and wallet relationships.

Holder Density and Holder Efficiency measure ownership quality.

They should remain separate but complementary.

==================================================
DESIGN GOAL
==================================================

The Security Briefing should clearly answer two separate questions:

1. Is ownership broadly distributed?
   - Holder Distribution
   - Holder Density
   - Holder Efficiency
   - Top Holder

2. Are holders secretly coordinated?
   - Bundle Concentration Risk
   - Launch Coordination Risk
   - Wallet Cluster Confidence
   - Self-Snipe Evidence

Both perspectives should contribute to the final Security Tier and overall risk assessment.
