ENTRY PATTERN ENGINE REHAUL

OBJECTIVE

Replace the current percentage-change-heavy pattern classification system with a structure-based classification system that leverages the existing Structural Analysis Engine.

The goal is to produce more accurate and meaningful Entry Pattern labels while reducing over-classification into:

- Dev Sell Absorption
- Second Pump Leg

========================================================
CURRENT PROBLEM
========================================================

Current pattern detection relies heavily on:

5m Change
1h Change
6h Change
24h Change

and uses first-match-wins logic.

This causes:

- Too many Dev Sell Absorption classifications
- Too many Second Pump Leg classifications
- Poor distinction between continuation and exhaustion
- Poor distinction between absorption and simple recovery

========================================================
NEW PRIORITY ORDER
========================================================

Patterns should be determined primarily from:

1. Shelf Type
2. Structure Sequence
3. Demand Return Score
4. Demand Expansion Score
5. Acceptance Score
6. Breakout Efficiency
7. Trend Structure
8. Dev Activity

Price change percentages should become supporting signals only.

========================================================
PATTERN:
SECOND PUMP LEG
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify genuine continuation after consolidation.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Re-Accumulation

AND

Structure Sequence = Uptrend → Shelf
OR
Pullback → Shelf

AND

Demand Return Score ≥ 70

AND

Demand Expansion Score ≥ 65

AND

Breakout Efficiency ≥ 60

Optional Boost:

24h change > 50%

Label:

Second Pump Leg

Confidence:

High

========================================================
PATTERN:
DEV SELL ABSORPTION
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify successful absorption of developer selling.

Requirements:

Dev Sold = Yes

AND

Demand Return Score ≥ 65

AND

Demand Expansion Score ≥ 65

AND

Acceptance Score ≥ 70

AND

Shelf Type ≠ Distribution

AND

Shelf Type ≠ Dead-Cat

Display:

Developer selling absorbed by demand.

Label:

Dev Sell Absorption

========================================================
PATTERN:
ACCUMULATION BREAKOUT
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify early-stage breakouts before major expansion.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Accumulation

AND

Acceptance Score ≥ 75

AND

Demand Return Score ≥ 65

AND

Breakout Efficiency ≥ 60

AND

Overhead Supply ≠ High

Label:

Accumulation Breakout

========================================================
PATTERN:
RE-ACCUMULATION CONTINUATION
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify established trends preparing for another move.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Re-Accumulation

AND

Acceptance Score ≥ 70

AND

Demand Return Score ≥ 70

AND

Demand Expansion Score ≥ 60

Label:

Re-Accumulation Continuation

========================================================
PATTERN:
FLOOR BOUNCE
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify recovery from weakness.

Requirements:

Demand Return Score improving

AND

Acceptance Score between 55 and 75

AND

Shelf Type ≠ Dead-Cat

AND

Trend Structure improving

Label:

Floor Bounce

========================================================
PATTERN:
V-REVERSAL
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify sharp reversals from significant weakness.

Requirements:

Recent strong decline detected

AND

Demand Expansion Score ≥ 75

AND

Bounce Velocity Score ≥ 75

AND

Demand Return Score improving rapidly

Label:

V-Reversal

========================================================
PATTERN:
DISTRIBUTION RISK
========================================================

Purpose:

Warn of likely exhaustion.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Distribution

OR

Parabolic Risk = True

AND

Demand Return Score < 60

Label:

Distribution Risk

Priority:

High

========================================================
PATTERN:
DEAD-CAT BOUNCE
========================================================

Purpose:

Warn of likely failed recovery.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Dead-Cat

AND

Demand Return Score < 50

AND

Downtrend

Label:

Dead-Cat Bounce

Priority:

Highest Warning

========================================================
PATTERN:
CONSOLIDATION BREAKOUT
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify coins that have spent time consolidating before beginning a potential expansion phase.

Requirements:

Shelf Type = Accumulation

OR

Shelf Type = Neutral Consolidation

AND

Acceptance Score ≥ 75

AND

Demand Return Score ≥ 65

AND

Breakout Efficiency ≥ 60

AND

Overhead Supply ≠ High

AND

Compression Detected

(ATR Compression OR SD Compression)

Confidence Boost:

Demand Expansion Score ≥ 70

Display:

Pattern:
Consolidation Breakout

Meaning:

Price has consolidated and built acceptance.
Demand is returning while resistance appears to be weakening.

========================================================
PATTERN:
DOUBLE BOTTOM RECOVERY
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify support levels that have been successfully defended twice.

Requirements:

Two significant lows detected.

Conditions:

Difference between Low #1 and Low #2 ≤ 3%

AND

Low #2 does not significantly undercut Low #1

AND

Demand Return Score improving

AND

Demand Expansion Score improving

AND

Bounce Velocity Score ≥ 50

Confirmation:

Breakout Efficiency ≥ 60

OR

Neckline Break detected

Display:

Pattern:
Double Bottom Recovery

Meaning:

Market has tested support twice and buyers appear to be defending the same area successfully.

========================================================
PATTERN:
FAILED BREAKOUT RISK
========================================================

Purpose:

Identify continuation attempts that are likely failing.

Requirements:

Breakout Efficiency < 40

AND

Demand Return Score declining

AND

One of:

High Overhead Supply

OR

Repeated Resistance Rejections

OR

Demand Expansion Score < 50

Display:

Pattern:
Failed Breakout Risk

Meaning:

Buyers are struggling to maintain moves above resistance.
Continuation probability is weakening.

Priority:

High Warning

========================================================

========================================================

Demand Expansion Score
Pattern Criteria Match %

========================================================
MULTI-PATTERN DETECTION
========================================================

Instead of stopping at the first matching pattern:

Evaluate all patterns.

Display:

Primary Pattern

and

Secondary Pattern (if applicable)

Examples:

Primary Pattern:
Re-Accumulation Continuation

Secondary Pattern:
Dev Sell Absorption

Or: 

Primary Pattern:
Consolidation Breakout

Secondary Pattern:
Dev Sell Absorption

Or:

Primary Pattern:
Re-Accumulation Continuation

Secondary Pattern:
Double Bottom Recovery

This prevents broad categories from absorbing all classifications.


NOTE: Don't allow more than two displayed patterns at once. If the engine starts showing 4–5 patterns simultaneously, the labels lose meaning. A Primary Pattern + Secondary Pattern model is usually the sweet spot for trading decisions.


========================================================
PATTERN EXPLANATION
========================================================
Every detected pattern must display the reasons it was selected.

Display:

Why this pattern was selected.

Example:

Pattern:
Second Pump Leg

Reasons:

✓ Re-Accumulation Shelf

✓ Demand Return 82

✓ Demand Expansion 77

✓ Breakout Efficiency 85%

✓ Uptrend → Shelf Structure

Or: 

Pattern:
Consolidation Breakout

Reasons:

✓ Acceptance Score = 84

✓ Demand Return Score = 78

✓ Breakout Efficiency = 82%

✓ Compression Detected

✓ Low Overhead Supply


This improves transparency and makes pattern classification easier to trust.
