STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS ENGINE REFINEMENTS

OBJECTIVE

Improve reliability of continuation-entry detection while reducing noise and false positives.

========================================================
IMPROVEMENT #1 — DEMAND EXPANSION STABILITY
========================================================

Current issue:

Demand Expansion uses:

Recent 15 candles

vs

Previous 15 candles

This can be overly sensitive to a single whale buy or short-term spike.

Required Change:

Create:

Short-Term Expansion Score

using:

Last 15 candles

vs

Previous 15 candles

Create:

Medium-Term Expansion Score

using:

Last 30 candles

vs

Previous 30 candles

Final Demand Expansion Score:

DemandExpansion =
(ShortTermExpansion × 0.70)
+
(MediumTermExpansion × 0.30)

Display:

Short-Term Expansion
Medium-Term Expansion
Final Demand Expansion

========================================================
IMPROVEMENT #2 — RUNNER POTENTIAL REWEIGHTING
========================================================

Current issue:

Runner Potential uses equal weighting.

Continuation setups should prioritize momentum and demand.

Use:

Demand Return Score = 25%

Demand Expansion Score = 25%

Acceptance Score = 20%

Breakout Efficiency = 15%

Overhead Supply Score = 10%

Shelf Type Score = 5%

Display full breakdown.

========================================================
IMPROVEMENT #3 — BREAKOUT CONFIDENCE
========================================================

Current issue:

1 successful breakout out of 1 attempt
and
5 successful breakouts out of 5 attempts

both score 100%.

This is misleading.

Required:

Create:

Attempt Confidence Modifier

Based on number of breakout attempts.

Example:

1 attempt = 60% confidence

2 attempts = 75%

3 attempts = 85%

4+ attempts = 100%

Final Breakout Efficiency:

BreakoutEfficiency =
RawEfficiency × AttemptConfidenceModifier

Display:

Attempts
Successful Breakouts
Raw Efficiency
Confidence Modifier
Final Efficiency

========================================================
IMPROVEMENT #4 — SHELF QUALITY SCORE
Current Issue:
The strongest shelf may still be weak.
Do NOT block trades solely because shelf quality is weak.
Instead:
Create:
Shelf Quality Score
Inputs:
Density Score
Defense Score
Volume Score
Recency Weight
Output:
0–100
Classifications:
85–100 = Strong Shelf
70–84 = Healthy Shelf
55–69 = Developing Shelf
40–54 = Weak Shelf
0–39 = Unproven Shelf
Display:
Shelf Quality Score
Shelf Quality Classification
======================================================== SHELF QUALITY IMPACT
Strong Shelf
No penalty
Healthy Shelf
No penalty
Developing Shelf
Minor Confidence Tier reduction
Weak Shelf
Moderate Confidence Tier reduction
Unproven Shelf
Major Confidence Tier reduction
Entry Checklist = FAIL for Shelf Quality
======================================================== ENTRY CHECKLIST INTEGRATION
Shelf Quality:
Strong Shelf = PASS
Healthy Shelf = PASS
Developing Shelf = CAUTION
Weak Shelf = CAUTION
Unproven Shelf = FAIL
======================================================== RUNNER POTENTIAL INTEGRATION
Shelf Quality should contribute to Runner Potential.
Strong and Healthy shelves increase Runner Potential.
Developing shelves have neutral impact.
Weak and Unproven shelves reduce Runner Potential.
======================================================== DISPLAY REQUIREMENTS
Display Shelf Quality prominently within Structural Analysis.
Example:
Shelf Quality: 82 / 100
Classification: Healthy Shelf
Impact:
No Confidence Penalty
