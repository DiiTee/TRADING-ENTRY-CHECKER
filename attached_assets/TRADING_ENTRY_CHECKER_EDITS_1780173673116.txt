
We need to completely refactor the current Floor, Shelf, Safe Entry, and Air Entry logic in the Trading Entry Checker app.

The current system incorrectly mixes:

- LP Floor
- Macro Floor
- Local Floor/Shelf

into one averaged floor value, which creates misleading “safe entries” and causes many Air entries.

We should stop averaging unrelated floor types and instead switch to a structure-based support model focused on price acceptance and consolidation behavior.

========================
GOALS OF THE NEW SYSTEM

The new system should:

1. Detect real structural support instead of wick lows.
2. Detect active shelves/consolidation zones.
3. Measure how far current price is from accepted structure.
4. Detect volatility compression before breakouts.
5. Reject Air entries automatically.

========================
IMPORTANT DEFINITIONS

SD = Standard Deviation

Standard Deviation measures how spread out prices are from the average.
Low SD = price is flat and compressed.
High SD = price is volatile and unstable.

ATR = Average True Range

ATR measures average candle volatility/range.
Low ATR = calm price action / consolidation.
High ATR = explosive or unstable movement.

Both SD and ATR should be used together to detect shelves and volatility compression.


VWAP = Volume Weighted Average Price
It answers:
"At what average price did most money actually trade?"
Formula:
VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / Σ(Volume)
Meaning:
Price above VWAP
Buyers are winning.
Most money is underwater below current price.
Bullish.
Price at VWAP
Neutral.
Fair value.
Price below VWAP
Most money bought higher.
Potential overhead sellers.
Bearish.
For meme coins:
Price holding above VWAP after a shelf is often one of the strongest continuation signals.


========================
REMOVE CURRENT LOGIC

REMOVE OR DEPRECATE:

- avgFloor calculation
- floor averaging logic
- LP Floor influence on Safe Entry
- current Air Entry formula comparing Local Floor to Macro Floor

The following logic is structurally flawed and should no longer be used:

avgFloor = average(lpFloor, macroFloorMC, localFloorMC)

and:

airEntry = localFloor > macroFloor * 1.175

These formulas mix unrelated support concepts and produce inaccurate entries.

NEW SUPPORT HIERARCHY

Treat each support type separately:

1. Active Shelf / Structural Floor
   
   - Main trading support
   - Most important for entries

2. Macro Floor
   
   - Major historical support
   - Longer timeframe context only

3. LP Floor
   
   - Catastrophic collapse floor
   - Rug/death floor only
   - NOT for entry calculation

The app should prioritize:
ACTIVE SHELF > MACRO FLOOR > LP FLOOR

========================
NEW ACTIVE SHELF DETECTION

DO NOT use:

- lowest wick
- lowest candle low
- recent minimum price

Instead, calculate:
PRICE ACCEPTANCE CLUSTERS

Algorithm:

1. Take the last 30–60 one-minute candles.
Use fixed multi-timeframe lookback windows for consistency and stability.

Suggested defaults:

- 1m timeframe:
  last 30 candles
  for micro shelf detection and entry timing

- 5m timeframe:
  last 12 candles
  for structural validation and support confirmation

- 15m timeframe:
  last 8 candles
  for macro floor and trend context

This creates stable and predictable structure analysis while still capturing:

- short-term consolidation
- medium-term acceptance
- broader market context


2. Divide the price range into small buckets/zones.

Example:
120k–123k
123k–126k
126k–129k

3. For each bucket, calculate:

- candle close count
- wick overlap count
- total time spent
- optional volume traded

4. The bucket with:

- highest density
- highest touch count
- highest sustained duration

becomes:
ACTIVE STRUCTURAL SHELF

This shelf is the real support zone.

The shelf should represent:
“where price was accepted”
NOT:
“the lowest wick”.


Bucket sizing should be dynamic rather than fixed.

Calculate bucket size relative to the recent lookback range.

Example:

lookbackRange =
lookbackHigh - lookbackLow

Suggested approach:

bucketSize =
lookbackRange / desiredBucketCount

Recommended:
8–15 buckets across the active range.

This keeps shelf detection proportional across:

- low-cap slow movers
- high-volatility expansions
- large market cap transitions

The goal is to avoid:

- overly fragmented shelves
- overly broad support zones
- distorted touch-count analysis

========================
ACTIVE SHELF CONDITION:

A shelf is valid ONLY IF:
1m:
low SD
low ATR
compression
tight clustering
AND
5m:
price acceptance exists
multiple closes inside same zone
no aggressive rejection
This removes many fake setups.

A shelf is only “confirmed” if:
1m shelf score is strong AND
5m shelf score is moderate or strong

If:
1m shelf strong BUT
5m structure weak
then classify as: Potential Fake Shelf / Air Risk

A shelf should only be considered valid if the consolidation/acceptance zone persists for a minimum duration.

Suggested minimums:

- 1m shelf: at least 8–12 candles
- 5m shelf: at least 3–5 candles

Very short pauses should not qualify as true shelves.


Multi-timeframe shelf analysis should account for timeframe lag and freshness differences.

Because:

- 1m reacts immediately
- 5m and 15m structures update more slowly

the system should weight recent price interaction heavily when evaluating higher-timeframe shelves.

Recent 1m behavior should influence:

- shelf freshness
- shelf decay
- active support validity

This prevents stale higher-timeframe shelves from remaining falsely “strong” during rapid market shifts or explosive breakouts.

The app should support both:

- static thresholds

OVERHEAD SUPPLY RISK

Add Overhead Supply Risk Detection.

Purpose:

Estimate potential selling pressure above current price.

Many coins fail because large amounts of price acceptance exist above current market cap.

This creates trapped participants who may sell into rebounds.

The system should estimate overhead resistance using structure data rather than wallet-level holder analysis.

DO NOT require holder tracking.

Use historical price acceptance instead.

---

OVERHEAD ACCEPTANCE ANALYSIS

Measure:

- Total candle closes above current price
- Total time spent above current price
- Number of accepted shelf zones above current price
- Density of price acceptance above current price

Large acceptance zones above current price indicate stronger overhead resistance.

BREAKOUT EFFICIENCY

Add Breakout Efficiency Detection.

Purpose:

Measure how effectively price converts breakout attempts into actual continuation.

Healthy runners tend to break resistance efficiently.

Weak or exhausted coins repeatedly fail at the same resistance level.

---

BREAKOUT ATTEMPT DETECTION

Identify:

- Local resistance zones
- Historical shelf highs
- Recent swing highs

Track every attempt to break above resistance.

A breakout attempt occurs when price enters the resistance zone or briefly exceeds it.

---

BREAKOUT RESULT ANALYSIS

For each breakout attempt classify:

SUCCESSFUL:

- Resistance broken
- Acceptance forms above resistance
- Price holds above breakout level

FAILED:

- Quick rejection

- Breakdown into previous shelf
